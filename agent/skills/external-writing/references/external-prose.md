# External Prose Rules

Lead with the object, the judgment, and the report's analytical increment within the first quarter. Introduce each unfamiliar concept before relying on it, preferably through an action or observable consequence rather than a dictionary definition.

Organize sections around the reader's next question. Let concepts arrive one at a time along a concrete process. A paragraph should advance one main relationship. Use short sentences for judgments and longer sentences for causes, examples, and transitions.

Avoid textbook sequencing: announce topic, define object, explain mechanism, summarize abstraction. Avoid the opposite failure as well: decorative metaphors, slang, fake intimacy, and absolute claims added to sound human. Natural prose comes from a knowledgeable author following the real problem with the reader.

Remove translationese, abstract actors, institutional verbs, repetitive contrast templates, unsupported bilingual parentheticals, and internal terms such as phase, axiom, rubric, or thesis catalog. Keep product names, protocols, API names, code identifiers, numbers, and URLs exact.

Editing feedback diagnoses a missing prerequisite; it is not automatically a new heading. After revision, a reader who never saw the feedback conversation must still understand why each explanation appears where it does.
